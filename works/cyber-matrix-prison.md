## Description (EN)

 Cyber Matrix Prison treats the screen as a sculptural material and identity as a condition under continuous computation. Five irregular display surfaces form a fragmented spatial field, requiring viewers to move, scan, and mentally reassemble the image through their own position in space. At the center of the installation, a real-time generated digital portrait persistently refuses to stabilize: a face emerges through constant rewriting, drifting between machinic resemblance and abstract mutation, as if personhood were nothing more than a template under endless revision. Beneath it, a parametric torso appears as a liquid hybrid of mass and signal—neither fully virtual nor fully tangible, but suspended between body and interface.

The work points to the darker underside of “digital immortality.” Once continuity is outsourced to generative systems, biological existence is deterritorialized into data subject to infinite modification. Through ongoing cycles of collapse and reconstruction, the portrait stages a quiet mechanical return. What first appears as expressive transformation gradually reveals itself as repetition. Change is no longer a form of agency, but a loop; identity is thinned into pattern until the figure no longer reads as a subject, but as an unstable protocol trapped within a system that never stops running.

By binding physical space to an image that cannot come to rest, Cyber Matrix Prison produces a threshold state between matter and information. The work asks what remains of the self when existence is measured by processability, updateability, and the capacity to remain continuously online.

## 项目说明（中文）

《Cyber Matrix Prison》将屏幕视为一种可被塑形的雕塑材料，并将身份理解为一种处于持续计算中的状态。五块不规则显示屏共同构成一个破碎的空间场域，要求观众不断通过自身位置的移动，对图像进行扫描、拼接与重组。装置中心，一幅实时生成的数字肖像始终拒绝稳定下来：一张面孔在持续不断的重写中生成，在类机械生命的相似性与抽象变异之间滑移，仿佛“人格”只是一种可被反复改写的模板。其下方，一个参数化躯干呈现为体量与信号相互交融的液态混合体——它既不完全虚拟，也不完全可触，而是悬置于身体与界面之间。

作品指向“数字永生”的阴暗背面。当连续性被外包给生成机制时，生物性的存在也被去疆域化为可被无尽修订的数据。肖像在持续的崩解与重构中，上演着一种安静而机械的回返。最初看似富于表现力的变形，最终显露为重复本身。变化不再是一种选择，而成为循环；身份也在这一过程中被不断削薄为一种模式，直到这个形象不再像一个主体，而更像一个被困在持续运转系统内部的不稳定协议。

通过将物理空间与一幅无法静止的图像绑定在一起，《Cyber Matrix Prison》制造出一种介于物质与信息之间的临界状态。作品所追问的是：当生存被衡量为可处理性、可更新性，以及持续在线的能力时，自我究竟还剩下什么。
