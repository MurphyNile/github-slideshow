# Bio

Murphy Nile (Ziling Zhou) is a transmedia artist with an MA in Computational Arts from Goldsmiths, University of London. Working across real-time 3D environments, machinima, and interactive audiovisual experiences, his practice constructs dystopian yet absurd digital landscapes that examine how attention is organized, desire is engineered, and alienation is quietly embedded in technologically driven social life.

His work is shaped by an ongoing interest in predictive logic, technological fantasy, and the ways history is reinterpreted through computational media. These concerns are developed through what he describes as a screen-based spatial dramaturgy: a method of staging ideas within digital environments. In his practice, the real-time engine is not treated as a neutral technical tool, but as both a mechanism of production and a medium that can be redirected, tested, and critically examined.

Murphy Nile was finalisted for the 2025 Lumen Prize Experiential Award and nominated for the UNESCO City of Media Arts Award at IDIF Karlsruhe. His works have been exhibited and screened internationally at venues and platforms including the Milan Machinima Festival, SUPERBOOTH 24 in Berlin, and the West Bund Museum in Shanghai. He has also presented at Unreal Fest (Epic Games, Shanghai) and the ISEA Symposium in Dubai, sustaining an active dialogue between artistic research, real-time engine practice, and critical theory.

## 简介

Murphy Nile（周子凌）是一位跨媒介艺术家，硕士毕业于伦敦大学金史密斯学院Computational Arts，创作横跨实时 3D 环境、Machinima 以及互动视听体验。他的实践构建出既反乌托邦又带有荒诞感的数字景观，探查注意力如何被编排、欲望如何被工程化，以及异化如何被悄然嵌入技术驱动的社会生活结构之中。他持续关注预测性逻辑、技术幻想，以及历史如何在计算媒介中被重新阐释。这些主题通过“基于屏幕的空间戏剧学”展开，一种在数字环境内部对思想进行舞台化呈现的方法；在他的实践中，实时引擎并非中性的技术工具，而是既作为生产机制本身，也作为可被反向调动和审视的媒介。

他曾入围 2025 年英国流明奖 Lumen Prize Experiential Award，并获 IDIF Karlsruhe 的 UNESCO City of Media Arts Award 提名。其作品曾在多个国际机构与平台展出及放映，包括 Milan Machinima Festival（米兰）、SUPERBOOTH 24（柏林）与西岸美术馆（上海）等，并曾于 Unreal Fest （Epic Games，上海）及 ISEA Symposium（迪拜）发表演讲，在艺术理论、实时引擎实践与艺术研究之间持续展开对话。
